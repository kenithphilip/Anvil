# Strategic Bet 07: BRSR value-chain reporting pack

> Source: research synthesis 2026-05-10. Companion to
> `docs/STRATEGIC_PLAN_2026_05.md` Bet 7.
> Status: research complete; 5-week MVP achievable for FY 2025-26
> voluntary disclosure window.

## TL;DR

Build two surfaces on one schema:

1. **Supplier surface**: Anvil tenants (tier-2 suppliers) fill out
   a 12-20 field BRSR Core ESG questionnaire once per period.
2. **Buyer surface**: listed-company buyer-tenants pull rolled-up
   BRSR-Core-shaped CSV / XBRL stub of their tier-2 suppliers'
   disclosures.

Pricing: supplier-side bundled in existing tiers; **buyer-side
charged at Rs 1,49,990/month base + Rs 1,500/supplier/period**.

Target: 5 enterprise pilots from listed-company tier within 12
months, each dragging 20-50 tier-2 suppliers onto Anvil.

Effort: ~23 engineering days + ~8 content days, ~5-7 calendar weeks.
Migration `097`. **MVP shippable mid-July 2026 to capture FY 2025-26
voluntary window before listed-company auditors lock the ledger in
Sept-Oct.**

## 1. Research summary

### 1.1 Current SEBI BRSR Core state (post 2025/42)

VERIFIED via SEBI primary circulars:

- Original [SEBI/HO/CFD/CFD-SEC-2/P/CIR/2023/122 (12 Jul 2023)](https://www.sebi.gov.in/legal/circulars/jul-2023/brsr-core-framework-for-assurance-and-esg-disclosures-for-value-chain_73854.html)
- Easing amendment [SEBI/HO/CFD/CFD-PoD-1/P/CIR/2025/42 (28 Mar 2025)](https://www.sebi.gov.in/legal/circulars/mar-2025/measures-to-facilitate-ease-of-doing-business-with-respect-to-framework-for-assurance-or-assessment-esg-disclosures-for-value-chain-and-introduction-of-voluntary-disclosure-on-green-credits_93102.html)

**Mandatory assurance/assessment phasing** by market-cap rank:
- Top 150: from FY 2023-24
- **Top 250: from FY 2024-25** (already in force)
- Top 500: from FY 2025-26
- **Top 1000: from FY 2026-27**

**Value-chain ESG disclosures**:
- **Voluntary for top 250 from FY 2025-26**.
- Assurance/assessment of value-chain disclosures: **voluntary for
  top 250 from FY 2026-27**.
- SEBI swapped "assurance" for "assurance or assessment" so an ISF-
  standard assessment satisfies the requirement at lower cost.

**Materiality threshold tightened**:
- Only partners that are **>= 2% of total purchases or sales by
  value**
- Companies allowed to cap disclosure at the **top partners
  cumulatively covering 75%** of purchases / sales.

### 1.2 The 9 BRSR Core attributes

The 9 attributes that need third-party assurance/assessment are
exactly the BRSR Core scope (no "9 of 12" subset; BRSR Core *is* the
9-attribute mandatory subset of the broader 140-indicator BRSR):

1. **GHG footprint** - Scope 1 + 2 (tCO2e), intensity per Rs revenue
   (raw and PPP-adjusted).
2. **Water footprint** - withdrawal (kL), consumption (kL), discharge
   by destination, intensity ratio.
3. **Energy footprint** - total (GJ), % renewable, intensity (per Rs
   revenue, PPP-adjusted).
4. **Embracing circularity (waste)** - generated by category (MT),
   recycled/reused (MT), sent to disposal (MT).
5. **Enabling gender diversity** - % women in workforce / KMP /
   board, POSH complaints, return-to-work after parental leave.
6. **Enabling inclusive development** - % input material from MSMEs
   / small producers / sourced within India; districts with social-
   impact assessments.
7. **Fairness in engaging with customers and suppliers** - anti-
   competitive complaints, % deductions/penalties on suppliers,
   data privacy / cybersecurity breaches.
8. **Open-ness of business** - concentration with related parties,
   share of RPT in purchases/sales, loans/advances to related
   parties as % of net worth.
9. **Gross wages paid to women + Job creation in smaller towns** -
   women wage / total wage ratio, job creation in tier 3-6 cities
   (% of wages paid to persons in smaller towns).

### 1.3 Disclosure schema

VERIFIED:
- SEBI publishes BRSR Core in **Annexure I as a tabular PDF** (8
  pages). [Annexure I PDF](https://www.sebi.gov.in/sebi_data/commondocs/jul-2023/Annexure_I-Format-of-BRSR-Core_p.pdf).
- Filed to NSE / BSE in PDF today; **XBRL tagging anticipated** but
  not yet mandatory.
- Schema: `Sr.No. | Attribute | Parameter | Unit | FY current | FY previous | Data & Approach`.

For Anvil's purposes:
- **Structured CSV mapping 1:1 to Annexure I rows** is the right
  interchange (mandatory P0).
- **XBRL stub** for forward-compatibility (placeholder namespace
  `urn:sebi:brsr-core:2025` until SEBI publishes the official
  taxonomy).

### 1.4 Vendor / competitor analysis

**Enterprise carbon/ESG platforms** (Sphera, Workiva, Watershed,
Persefoni, SAP Sustainability Footprint Management, Microsoft
Sustainability Manager, IBM Envizi):
- All are oriented at the *listed buyer*, not the tier-2 supplier.
- SAP SFM uses [Sustainability Data Exchange (PACT V2)](https://www.sap.com/products/business-network/sustainability-data-exchange.html)
  for supplier-to-buyer footprint.
- [Microsoft Sustainability Manager ESG value chain solution](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/industry/sustainability/sustainability-manager-esg-value-chain-solution)
  ships a Power Pages portal supplier survey.
- Pricing: enterprise-only; Sphera $60-200k/yr (Vendr).

**Supplier-engagement specialists**:
- [EcoVadis](https://ecovadis.com/plans-pricing/): assessment
  ratings, $2.7-3k/yr per supplier "Select"; Tier 1+2 add-on
  $31,500/yr for 100 assessments.
- Sourcemap: transparency / due-diligence.
- NQC SAQ, Achilles: sector questionnaires.

**Indian-specific**:
- **Updapt** (Bangalore) - closest direct competitor: ESG SaaS with
  value-chain partner module, climate-risk module, BRSR-aligned
  reporting.
- Treeni, Sprih, Breathe ESG, NeoImpact, Glocert, NCMA, Zevero - all
  India-focused BRSR consultancies / SaaS.

**Where Anvil's niche sits**: Updapt is the only India-native ESG
SaaS with a supplier portal, but it's **sold top-down to listed
companies that pay Updapt to onboard suppliers** - which means each
supplier sees a different vendor's UI per buyer they serve. The
**supplier-side product** (one Anvil tenant fills out one BRSR Core
form per period and shares it with multiple listed buyers) is the
gap. Anvil's tier-2 suppliers already have a tenant; bolt the BRSR
Core form onto their existing console and the marginal CAC is zero.

## 2. Recommended approach

Two surfaces, one schema:

### 2.1 Supplier surface (Anvil tenant fills it once per period)

A 12-field single-screen "ESG Disclosure" form under a new top-level
nav `brsr` for tenants who toggle it on. Pre-fills from the prior
period; supports draft / submit / lock; computes Scope 1/2 from
typed-in fuel and electricity volumes via stored India emission
factors; takes attestation by an authorized signer. Default cadence:
**annual (FY ending 31-Mar)** plus optional FY-quarterly for buyers
who require quarterly data.

### 2.2 Buyer surface (listed-company buyer-tenant)

A new `brsr-buyer-dashboard` screen showing the listed company's
tier-2 supplier coverage: which Anvil-tenant suppliers have linked
themselves to this buyer (via existing `source_pos`/`orders`
linkage), their >=2% / 75% materiality status, period status, total
Scope 3 rollup, and a one-click BRSR-Core CSV + XBRL export.
**The buyer pays.**

### 2.3 Pricing model

| Tier | Price |
|---|---|
| **Supplier-tenant** (already on a paid Anvil tier) | Free, bundled |
| **Supplier-only** (new tenants who only want BRSR) | Rs 4,990/month sub-tier (1 user, 2 periods in flight) |
| **Buyer-tenant** | Rs 1,49,990/month base (sits above today's Rs 99,990 Enterprise) + Rs 1,500/supplier/period |
| **Buyer-tenant "BRSR Plus"** | Includes assurance-evidence trail + annual auditor-pack export |

Listed company with 40 tier-2 suppliers tracked: 1,49,990 + 40 *
1,500 = Rs 2,09,990/month.

Compares favourably to EcoVadis (Rs 2,60,000/yr per supplier
rating); Anvil is roughly half on a per-supplier basis at
Rs 18,000/year.

Rationale: the supplier already pays for the base platform;
charging them again creates the carrot/stick problem and nukes
adoption. The listed buyer gets the regulatory value, so they pay.

## 3. Data model + migrations

**Migration `097_brsr_value_chain.sql`** (re-number if other 097s
land first). Three new tables, idempotent, RLS on every table.

```sql
-- 1. supplier_disclosure_periods: FY/period definition per supplier-tenant.
create table if not exists supplier_disclosure_periods (
  id uuid primary key default uuid_generate_v4(),
  tenant_id uuid not null references tenants(id) on delete cascade,
  fiscal_year text not null,            -- "FY2025-26"
  cadence text not null check (cadence in ('annual','quarterly')),
  period_start date not null,
  period_end date not null,
  status text not null default 'open'
    check (status in ('open','submitted','locked','assured')),
  submitted_at timestamptz,
  locked_at timestamptz,
  attestation_user_id uuid references auth.users(id),
  attestation_text text,
  created_at timestamptz not null default now(),
  updated_at timestamptz not null default now(),
  unique (tenant_id, fiscal_year, cadence, period_start)
);

-- 2. supplier_disclosures: one row per supplier-tenant per period.
create table if not exists supplier_disclosures (
  id uuid primary key default uuid_generate_v4(),
  tenant_id uuid not null references tenants(id) on delete cascade,
  period_id uuid not null references supplier_disclosure_periods(id) on delete cascade,
  -- Environment (P6, BRSR Core attributes 1-4)
  scope1_tco2e numeric(14,3),
  scope2_tco2e numeric(14,3),
  electricity_kwh numeric(14,2),
  electricity_renewable_pct numeric(5,2),
  diesel_litres numeric(14,2),
  petrol_litres numeric(14,2),
  natural_gas_scm numeric(14,2),
  water_withdrawal_kl numeric(14,2),
  water_consumption_kl numeric(14,2),
  waste_total_mt numeric(14,3),
  waste_recycled_mt numeric(14,3),
  -- Social (P3, P5, attributes 5,9)
  women_pct_workforce numeric(5,2),
  women_pct_kmp numeric(5,2),
  posh_complaints int,
  ehs_lost_time_injuries int,
  ehs_fatalities int,
  gross_wages_inr numeric(16,2),
  wages_paid_to_women_inr numeric(16,2),
  wages_paid_smaller_towns_inr numeric(16,2),
  -- Inclusive development & open-ness (attributes 6,7,8)
  msme_input_pct numeric(5,2),
  india_sourcing_pct numeric(5,2),
  related_party_purchases_pct numeric(5,2),
  -- Compliance attestations
  pollution_consent_valid boolean,
  factory_act_compliant boolean,
  -- Free-form for fields outside the schema
  extra jsonb not null default '{}'::jsonb,
  created_at timestamptz not null default now(),
  updated_at timestamptz not null default now(),
  unique (tenant_id, period_id)
);

-- 3. value_chain_relationships: supplier-tenant <-> buyer-tenant.
create table if not exists value_chain_relationships (
  id uuid primary key default uuid_generate_v4(),
  supplier_tenant_id uuid not null references tenants(id) on delete cascade,
  buyer_tenant_id uuid not null references tenants(id) on delete cascade,
  relationship_type text not null check (relationship_type in ('upstream','downstream')),
  buyer_purchase_share_pct numeric(5,2),
  is_material boolean generated always as
    (buyer_purchase_share_pct >= 2) stored,
  consent_status text not null default 'pending'
    check (consent_status in ('pending','accepted','rejected','revoked')),
  consent_at timestamptz,
  created_at timestamptz not null default now(),
  updated_at timestamptz not null default now(),
  unique (supplier_tenant_id, buyer_tenant_id, relationship_type)
);
```

Plus seed table **`india_emission_factors`** (global, tenant_id null)
with:
- CEA grid factor FY 2024-25: **0.710 tCO2/MWh** per
  [CEA Baseline Database v21.0, Nov 2025](https://cea.nic.in/wp-content/uploads/baseline/2025/12/User_Guide_V_21.0.pdf).
- DEFRA diesel/petrol factors per
  [DESNZ/DEFRA 2025](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/greenhouse-gas-reporting-conversion-factors-2025).
- IPCC default GWPs.

Refreshed via CEA cron once a year. `scope2_tco2e` populates from
`electricity_kwh * factor * (1 - renewable_pct/100)` server-side at
submit-time.

RLS is the standard pattern plus a special policy on
`value_chain_relationships` allowing the buyer-tenant to read
supplier rows once `consent_status='accepted'`.

## 4. User-visible UX

### 4.1 Supplier-side (`/v3#brsr-supplier`)

- One scrollable screen, ~12 visible inputs with smart defaults
  and tooltips. Sections: Energy, Water/Waste, People, Compliance.
- Period selector at top; "Copy from FY 2024-25" button does prior-
  period prefill (required - SMEs hate re-entering).
- Live-computed Scope 1 / Scope 2 figure in the header.
- Industry-default presets: "Auto components - Tamil Nadu" prefills
  electricity-grid factor and typical water-use ratio.
- Estimation rules: if `electricity_kwh` blank but `electricity_inr`
  filled, derive kWh at state-tariff lookup. If `diesel_litres`
  blank but tenant runs Anvil's logistics module, sum from
  `shipments` rows.
- Mobile-friendly: stack at <768px, big tap targets, no multi-
  column forms.
- Draft auto-saves every 30s. Submit triggers TOTP step-up if MFA
  enabled.
- Attestation: signer types name + role + checks "I attest" box;
  signature persisted to `supplier_disclosure_periods.attestation_*`.

### 4.2 Buyer-side (`/v3#brsr-buyer-dashboard`)

- Top KPI strip: total tier-2 suppliers connected, % of >=2%
  material suppliers reporting, % of 75% cumulative-purchase-share
  covered, total Scope 3 (rolled-up) for the FY.
- Suppliers table: name, FY share %, material flag, period status,
  last submitted, attestation signer. Click-through to read-only
  supplier disclosure with assurance-evidence panel.
- Two export buttons: **BRSR Core CSV** and **BRSR Core XBRL stub**.
  Both pull from `/api/brsr/buyer/export`.
- Filters: FY, cadence, material-only.
- Coverage gauge: red until >= 75% cumulative purchases reached;
  amber 60-75%; green 75%+. SEBI's threshold lives here.

### 4.3 Assurance evidence trail

Per-disclosure "Audit pack" link bundling typed-in attestation,
source documents (linked from existing `documents` table -
electricity bills, water meter readings, payroll register),
immutable change-log via existing `audit_events_append_only`
(migration 058).

## 5. Technical implementation plan

### 5.1 New API module `src/api/brsr/`

- `period.js` - `GET /api/brsr/period?fy=...` returns the period
  for the supplier. `POST` creates a new period. Annual auto-roll
  on **1 April** via a new daily-cron entry.
- `disclosure.js` - `GET /api/brsr/disclosure` reads current period
  row. `POST` upserts; computes derived fields server-side using
  `_lib/brsr-emission-factors.js`. `POST /api/brsr/disclosure/submit`
  locks the row and writes `audit_events_append_only`.
- `prefill.js` - `GET /api/brsr/disclosure/prefill?from_fy=...`
  returns prior-period values + module-derived defaults.
- `relationship.js` - `POST /api/brsr/relationships/invite` lets a
  buyer invite a supplier-tenant by email; `POST /api/brsr/relationships/accept`
  is the supplier-side consent.
- `buyer/export.js` - `GET /api/brsr/buyer/export?format=csv|xbrl&fy=...`
  produces BRSR-Core-shaped CSV (one row per supplier per attribute)
  or XBRL instance per (forthcoming) SEBI taxonomy. CSV is mandatory
  P0; XBRL is a stub returning a documented "not yet finalized by
  SEBI" header.
- `buyer/dashboard.js` - `GET /api/brsr/buyer/dashboard` returns
  coverage stats, materiality table, Scope 3 rollup. Server-side
  >=2% / 75% computation by joining `value_chain_relationships` to
  `source_pos` revenue (or `orders` if upstream).

### 5.2 New screens

- `src/v3-app/screens/brsr-supplier.tsx` (+ `.test.tsx`) - the
  12-field form.
- `src/v3-app/screens/brsr-buyer-dashboard.tsx` (+ `.test.tsx`) -
  the buyer rollup.
- `src/v3-app/screens/brsr-disclosure-detail.tsx` - read-only
  detail view (audit trail) used by both buyer and supplier.
- Add three entries to `src/v3-app/routes.ts` and the v3 nav.
- All four reuse `Btn`, `Card`, `KPI`, `Chip` primitives and follow
  the V3_WIRING_PATTERN.md convention.

### 5.3 RBAC

Extend `src/v3/rbac.js` with:
- `brsr:supplier:edit` (sales_engineer, admin)
- `brsr:supplier:submit` (admin only)
- `brsr:buyer:read` (admin, finance, sales_manager)
- `brsr:buyer:export` (admin, finance)

### 5.4 Cron

- `brsr/period_roll` to `/api/cron/daily` (runs 02:30 UTC daily;
  acts only on 1-April).
- `brsr/refresh_emission_factors` annually around 1 December when
  CEA publishes the new baseline.

### 5.5 Export endpoints

- **CSV**: column ordering of Annexure I:
  `Sr.No. | Attribute | Parameter | Unit | FY current | FY previous | Approach`.
  One row per supplier per attribute, with a buyer-summary row at
  the foot.
- **XBRL**: emit a documented "stub" instance with namespace
  placeholder `urn:sebi:brsr-core:2025` until SEBI publishes the
  official taxonomy. Buyer can submit CSV via NSE/BSE's PDF/upload
  workflow today; XBRL switch is ready for when SEBI flips it.

### 5.6 Tests

- vitest unit tests for emission-factor math
  (`brsr-emission-factors.test.js`).
- Materiality-rollup math.
- Period auto-roll.
- Integration test through `npm run check` and `npm run audit`.

### 5.7 Audit script update

Extend `src/scripts/audit-screens-deep.mjs` and
`audit-data-model.mjs` to recognise the new tables and screens so
`npm run audit` continues to pass.

## 6. Risks and open questions

1. **SEBI schema lock risk (HIGH)**. SEBI has not finalized an XBRL
   taxonomy for BRSR Core. Annexure I PDF is canonical; field
   labels have shifted twice (Jul 2023 -> Jan 2025 -> Mar 2025).
   Mitigation: ship CSV first, keep XBRL behind a feature flag,
   version the export endpoint (`/api/brsr/buyer/export/v1`), store
   BRSR Core attribute IDs as enum-like text values so a schema
   bump becomes a seed update.
2. **Assurance vendor partnership**. "Assurance" requires an
   empaneled CA firm or ISF-certified assessor. Anvil should partner
   with **2-3 mid-market Indian assurance firms** (DNV, BDO, Grant
   Thornton Bharat) for a referral revenue split. Open: pre-empanel
   any provider, or stay neutral?
3. **Tier-2 supplier adoption (carrot vs stick)**. Soft adoption
   fails because the supplier sees no upside. Realistic path: the
   listed buyer **mandates** "use Anvil for BRSR" as a condition of
   vendor onboarding; the supplier gets the form free as part of
   their existing Anvil tenancy. Need 1-2 listed-buyer pilots to set
   precedent. Sales conversation, not engineering.
4. **PII / wage data sharing**. `wages_paid_to_women_inr` is
   sensitive. Supplier shares only with consented buyers via
   `value_chain_relationships.consent_status='accepted'` gate.
   Should also offer a "summary mode" returning ratios
   (`women_wage_pct`) instead of absolute INR for buyers who need
   only the BRSR Core *intensity* metric.
5. **Multi-buyer same-supplier**. A tier-2 supplier may serve five
   listed buyers. Disclosure is one row per period, shared with N
   buyers. Schema supports this. UX must make the consent step
   explicit per buyer.
6. **Scope 3 rollup correctness**. "Total Scope 3" for the buyer is
   *not* the sum of supplier Scope 1+2 figures - it is the buyer's
   spend-weighted attribution of supplier emissions. Rollup math
   must apply spend-share weighting, not naive summation. Surface
   this caveat in the UI to avoid auditor pushback.
7. **Top 250 -> top 1000 segmentation**. FY 2026-27 expansion to
   top 1000 means buyer TAM grows 4x in 12 months. Make sure
   pricing/tier mechanics scale (no per-supplier price floor that
   breaks at higher counts).
